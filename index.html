About Me

Hi, my name is Jakub Berut.
I am:

  • Game Developer since 2019
  • Graduate of Applied Computer Science at the Technical University of Lodz with a specialization in Game Development and Computer Simulation
  • Member of the DVD Unicorns team
  • Leader of the team that won the 2022 ZTGK Challenge in the Game Development category

Here you can find some of my professional and personal projects. For more details on my professional work, take a look at my résumé or LinkedIn profile. I am a quick learner who loves to constantly explore new concepts and ideas (sometimes falling into various rabbit holes). While working with designers, I always try to look at ideas from both the technical and user sides to provide the best experience that works the best given existing systems and limitations.

Underdose – Big Cheese Studio

01.2023 - 07.2023

In a not-too-distant, retro-future world shaken by a shortage of medicine, a talented pharmacist is accused of murdering Patient Zero. He flees to the slums, where he tries to survive in isolation until he can recreate the formula for the drug that will save his life.

In Underdose, I was mainly a Game Programmer. I was responsible for prototyping and implementing gameplay mechanics, creating gameplay-related systems and developing needed tools. Personal Projects

Bytebond – DVD Unicorns

07.2022 - Current

Bytebond is an asymmetric co-op puzzle game that takes place inside the computer. Bond with your friend and become a part of the Antivirus Special Forces as you head to the heart of the PC. Solve electrifying puzzles to regain control over the infected CPU. The power conundrum awaits!

Our game has been exhibited at events such as
Game Access 2022 (won "Best Gameplay" at Indie Showcase),
ZTGK Contest 2022 (won in "Game Design" category),
PGA 2022, and Digital Dragons 2023.

The game is developed in Unity. In out team I am the main Gameplay Programmer. Most of the time, I am responsible for creating prototypes and, later, final versions of various gameplay mechanics. During our design meetings, I try to discuss whether the presented ideas are feasible, coherent and, most importantly, fun for the player.

Delayed – Skybox Is The Limit

03.2022 – 06.2022

Delayed is a 3D puzzle-platformer where you will play as a shipwrecked astronaut who is followed by his double from the past. With his help, you will traverse the planet of levitating islands, discover the truth about the time anomaly occurring there and retrieve missing parts of your broken spaceship.

Winner of 2022 ZTGK Contest in the Game Development Category with special awards from:

  • SuperHot
  • Bloober Team
  • Infinity Ward

Delayed was created with our custom game engine coded in C++ using OpenGL as the render API. It was developed by a team of six (3 programmers, 2 artists, and a level designer) in ~4 months. In our team I was a Team Leader, one of Programmers and I was assisting with Game Design.

Make Me Mime

02.2024

Global Game Jam 2024 Entry!

Make Me Mime is a power fantasy first-person shooter game where you play as a mime. Unleash your creativity thorough an imaginary handgun and make people laugh. By shooting with your imagination, bring joy to the world around you. Get more and more joyful after time, and soon you might understand the real truth about you.

Awarded 2nd place at Łódzki Game Jam 8 jam site!

This game was created in ~48 hours by a team of 5 (2 programmers, 2 artists, and a designer) in Unity. In this project I was responsible for developing the gameplay of this game. I created movement and shooting. I added enemies and a score system. I also made the whole game sequence and environment details. The second developer, among others, created vfx, sfx system and blood system.

Slash

08.2023

Project created with Unreal Engine 5 C++ The Ultimate Game Developer Course from Udemy. Developed solo in 2 weeks to get the hang of the Unreal Engine 5 and its differences from Unity.

What I learned:

  • Unreal Engine fundamentals
  • Creating gameplay elements using C++ and blueprints
  • Creating basic SFX and VFX
  • Implementing basic enemy logic using the behavior tree
  • Using animation blueprints and implementing basic IK
  • The fact that in many areas, core concepts are not different between engines

Camera Defuser

01.2022

In Camera Defuser, you play as a camera drone following his robot body. Help a small robot defuse a bomb by avoiding obstacles and interacting with levels by controlling the camera.

Developed in Unity by a team of two (a programmer and designer) as a proof of concept for the Fundamentals of Computer Games subject at the university.

As a programmer, I implemented all the gameplay mechanics (movement, proximity indicators, unique mechanic: what is usually used as a camera to show what is on the screen plays a huge role and is a physical object in the game world).

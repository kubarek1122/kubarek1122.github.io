About Me

Hi, my name is Jakub Berut.
I am:

  • Game Developer since 2019
  • Graduate of Applied Computer Science at the Technical University of Lodz with a specialization in Game Development and Computer Simulation
  • Member of the DVD Unicorns team
  • Leader of the team that won the 2022 ZTGK Challenge in the Game Development category

Here you can find some of my personal projects. For professional work, take a look at my résumé or LinkedIn profile. I am a quick learner who loves to constantly explore new concepts and ideas (sometimes falling into various rabbit holes). While working with designers, I always try to look at ideas from both the technical and user sides to provide the best experience that works the best given existing systems and limitations.

Bytebond – DVD Unicorns

07.2022 - Current

Bytebond is an asymmetric co-op puzzle game that takes place inside the computer. Bond with your friend and become a part of the Antivirus Special Forces as you head to the heart of the PC. Solve electrifying puzzles to regain control over the infected CPU. The power conundrum awaits!

Developed in Unity by the DVD Unicorns team where I am mainly a Gameplay Programmer but each of us performs more than one function.

Delayed

03.2022 – 06.2022

Delayed is a 3D puzzle-platformer where you will play as a shipwrecked astronaut who is followed by his double from the past. With his help, you will traverse the planet of levitating islands, discover the truth about the time anomaly occurring there and retrieve missing parts of your broken spaceship.

Winner of 2022 ZTGK Contest in the Game Development Category!
Special awards from:

  • SuperHot
  • Bloober Team
  • Infinity Ward

Delayed was created with our custom game engine coded in C++ using OpenGL as the render API. Developed by a team of six (3 programmers, 2 artists, and a designer) in ~4 months. I was a team leader and one of the programmers.

Slash

08.2023

Project created with Unreal Engine 5 C++ The Ultimate Game Developer Course. Developed solo in 2 weeks to get the hang of the Unreal Engine and its differences from Unity.

What I learned:

  • Unreal Engine fundamentals
  • Creating gameplay elements using C++ and blueprints
  • Creating basic SFX and VFX
  • Implementing basic enemy logic using the behavior tree
  • Using animations and implementing basic IK
  • In many areas, core concepts do not differ between engines

Space Invaders

07.2022

Small Space Invaders clone made in Unity. Created solo in one week as a recruitment task. The main features are:

  • State machine based game flow management
  • Object pooling for enemies and projectiles
  • Event driven UI
  • Customizable enemies through scriptable objects

Camera Defuser

01.2022

In Camera Defuser, you play as a camera drone following his robot body. Help a small robot defuse a bomb by avoiding obstacles and interacting with levels by controlling the camera.

Developed in Unity by a team of two (a programmer and designer) as a proof of concept for the Fundamentals of Computer Games subject at the university.

As a programmer, I implemented all the gameplay mechanics (movement, proximity indicators, unique mechanic: what is usually used as a camera to show what is on the screen plays a huge role and is a physical object in the game world).
